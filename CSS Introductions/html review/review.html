In this page I will be talking about how I discovered my love for minature models.
So these days I've been attending an afterschool program called the Emily Carr
Headstart in art District program.

We are currently on our final project. For my first two projects we did:

  1. Shading still life objects
  2. Study of human anatomy

And for our third project we are now doing space in 3D. Basically we look for scratches marks ar anything that looks strange around us, take a picture as evidence create a story about how that mark happened and make a model for the setting. Our teacher said we could do our models with paper, clay or pretty
much anything.

Now I barley have any expereince with 3d models, so I went to pinterest for some inspiration.


Link to picture ↑

I found these pictures of 3d minature models and wanted to do something similar to these.
I decide to make a minature 3d model of moffit park.I did not created the whole thing
though I cropped out a part of the park.

So I watched some tutorials at youtube and became facinated and excited about making it. And after that I bought the supplies to create my model (they were pretty expensive).
I am creating the model right now and it's really fun. I kind of feel like when god created the universe he created this and that, it's like I sand box game where I create things how I want and mabye that's why I'm having fun. I had three weeks to make this, so here what I did.

Week What I did
#1 Nov 5-11 I did the storyboard for the evidence.
#2 Nov 12-18 I did some field study at the park like sketches of how the park looked like and started making the model.
#3 Nov 19-25

I continued to create the model. Here's the materials I used to create the model.

  • 2 6"x11" styrofoam boards
  • Acrylic paint(brown,yellow,white,black,light green,dark green)
  • grass texture powder
  • minature fake trees
  • masking tape
  • play-doh clay

Once I finish my model I might post a picture of it here.
